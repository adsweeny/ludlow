39. Elizabeth Ludlow

In Minchinhampton parish register, Elizabeth's christening took place on January 4, 1852; parents abode was in Hampton Field and James was listed as "labourer" (1099, page 138). On the same day, her cousin, Sarah Ann Ludlow (g2), the daughter of William and Eliza Ludlow was christened in the parish.
In the 1861 England census, Elizabeth is listed as a 10 year old niece and "Scholar" staying at the home Thomas and Hannah Ludlow (her uncle and aunt) in Minchinhampton. In '71 census of Minchinhampton, Elizabeth, age 20, is listed as a "Stick Varnisher" and niece at Thomas Ludlow's place (along with a 2 year old "nephew" to Thomas, by the name of George Ludlow who could be an unknown youngest brother in the James Ludlow family or, perhaps, a child of Elizabeth. A "George Ludlow" born 1869 +/- 2 yrs in Gloucs is not in CRI index.)
She is not in the 1881 or 1891 England census under Elizabeth Ludlow.
Elizabeth LUDLOW married James HUMPHREYS in the first quarter of 1872. Listed as Elizabeth "Humphreys" in '81 census; as Elizabeth "Humphries" in '91 census, WHERE she is living NEXT DOOR to FANNY KING (her next younger sister!)!

James Humphries

Surname also spelled as HUMPHREYS or HUMPHRIS (esp. in earlier records).
In 1881 England census at "Peaches Farm" in the hamlet of Hampton Field, Minchinhampton, Gloucs, James is listed as an "Umbrella Stick Worker" along with his wife, Elizabeth, and three children. In '91 census at Hampton Field, Minchinhampton, he has the same occupation, wife Elizabeth and five children.
James married Susannah Workman Cuff in the 3rd quarter of 1899. In the 1901 census, he is shown as a "Stickworker" in Hampton Field living near George Ludlow (jml:e4a), his deceased wife's oldest son, and with Susannah and four of his children and two of Susannah's sons. James and Susanna had a son, Fred, born abt 1899.

170. Ralph Humphries

Ralph is listed as Ralph "Humphris", age 17, born in Hampton Fields (part of Minchinhampton), in the 1901 census. He was a boarder with the Frederick Smith family in the city of Gloucester and worked as an "Railroad Engine Cleaner."
